# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One person outdoors or at a windowsill with a plant in front of them, phone in one hand. They want to know what the plant is, then keep it. The session is seconds long and repeats often; the collection is revisited at leisure.

## Product Purpose

Floradex identifies plants from photos and collects them as numbered pixel-art dex entries with care details. The hero loop is capture, reveal, collect. Everything else is allowed to be merely good. Success is the reveal feeling worth repeating a hundred times and a collection worth scrolling.

## Brand Personality

Field guide crossed with creature collector. Curious, honest, a little playful; never childish, never clinical. Identification is probabilistic and the app says so in field-guide language ("looking, comparing, deciding"), never "processing". Numbers are permanent and treated with ceremony.

## Anti-references

- Generic scanner apps: cold utility chrome, spinners, fake precision percentages.
- Retro as costume: pixel fonts everywhere, CRT filters on chrome, arcade sounds. The sprites are the retro anchor; chrome converses with them in small doses.
- AI-default polish: accent-flooded screens, stacked cards with colliding radii, dark-mode-first drama, decorative glassmorphism.

## Design Principles

1. One overbuilt loop: the reveal is the only orchestrated sequence in the app, because the wait for identification is the reveal. Everywhere else, motion conveys state in one beat.
2. Honest intelligence: confidence bands, source agreement, and failure copy are content, not decoration. Never fake certainty for aesthetics.
3. Retro as accent: pixel art lives in the content layer (sprites, dex numbers). System chrome stays native Liquid Glass.
4. Physical first: haptics at semantic boundaries only; one signature spring; transforms during gestures, layout after.
5. Numbers are sacred: a dex number is assigned once, never reused, and the design makes it feel earned.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

System Dynamic Type for all body text; a display face only for numbers and headers. Reduce Motion collapses staged sequences to crossfades. Semantic colors with light and dark support; light mode is the design driver. One-handed outdoor use: controls in the thumb arc, legible in sunlight.
